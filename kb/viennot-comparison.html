Catenable deques: kaplan2 vs Viennot et al.

Qualitative comparison and head-to-head benchmark of two mechanized Kaplan–Tarjan (JACM 1999, §6) catenable deques · Fri, 12 Jun 2026 08:17:00 +0200

TL;DR. Two complete mechanizations of KT99 §6 exist side by side: our extrinsic rebuild (rocq/KTDeque/Catenable/, keystone closed 2026-06-11) and Viennot/Wendling/Guéneau/Pottier's intrinsic development. Functional verification: parity. Mechanized worst-case cost bound: ours only. Production wall-clock: our verified artifact (KTf — the OpsFast mirror over verified §4-deque buffers) is faster than Viennot's hand-written cadeque on 6 of the 9 workloads (eject-drain, mixed, all three concat patterns — up to 1.9× on concat-heavy — and persistent forks), at parity on pop-drain; their build-side push/inject remain ~1.4× ahead, a gap that lives inside the §4 buffer's push path.

2 · Qualitative comparison — five differences that matter

2.1 Where the invariant lives

Their GADT indices make ill-coloured structures unrepresentable: a mis-bundled packet simply does not typecheck, so a whole class of bugs is impossible before any proof is written. The price is rigidity — the mutually-indexed type families freeze the design, and refining an invariant means re-indexing every constructor and function. Our J is one Prop that was refined in place twice during discharge (the top-green clause, then the level-stratification clause) without touching a single operation. The rebuild methodology — freeze the ops first, grow the invariant under proof pressure — is only possible extrinsically.

2.2 What failing proofs buy

Because our ops are untyped until J speaks, three genuine operation bugs (concat's childless small-side lift, the pair-collapse re-crowning in pop/eject, and repair's double-degradation) survived until the corresponding lemmas refused to close — the proofs acted as the type checker. In their development the same bugs are caught earlier, by the indices. Net: intrinsic catches structure bugs at definition time; extrinsic catches them at proof time but tolerates a moving design.

2.3 Proof-text economy

Their cadeque theory is ~5× smaller than ours. Equations, dependent pattern matching and hauto discharge the bookkeeping that we do by hand in ~7,000 lines of structured Ltac. In exchange we depend on nothing but the kernel and stdlib — no plugin version pinning, and every proof is a readable forward script (the maintainability bet recorded in ADR-0004).

2.4 The cost claim — our main content differentiator

The Kaplan–Tarjan hard rule (worst-case, not amortized) is a theorem here: cat_wc_o1 counts buffer primitives along every branch of the frozen ops. VWGP's complexity claim lives in their paper's prose; their mechanization scope is functional correctness. And the buffer-primitives-to-wall-clock link is now real on our side too: the production artifact (KTf) implements every counted primitive with the verified §4 deque, so the mechanized constants translate directly into the flat lines in the charts below.

2.5 The port that proves itself

The production artifact is not a hand-written port: OpsFast.v mirrors every frozen operation against ~15 named buffer primitives, and each mirror carries an op_f = op equality lemma — the machine-checked diff of the port. The keystone bundle transfers verbatim (FastKeystone.v, six theorems, Print Assumptions closed). The only trusted seam is 17 one-line Extract Constant directives mapping the primitives to Fastbuf (= kt4 + a size field), each implementing a one-line list definition with an already-verified deque operation.

2.6 Statement-strength parity

Both developments state concat on two arbitrary regular deques (the clause our archived Cadeque9 could not even state), both close pop/eject with repair, and both have a clean axiom audit. At the level of "what is mechanically known about KT99 §6 functional behaviour", the two developments are equivalent.

3.3 Reading the results honestly

KTf beats Viennot on 6 of 9 workloads, flat at every size. At n = 10⁶: eject-drain 70 vs 77, mixed 58 vs 72, concat-fold 646 vs 1154 (1.8×), concat-tree 2649 vs 3497, concat+pop interleave 143 vs 269 (1.9×), and the persistent-fork rerun 53 vs 64 ns/op; pop-drain sits at parity (83 vs 83). The model layer's quadratic cells are gone: the verified buffer swap turned every (>cap) into a flat line.
Where we still lose: the two build-side workloads. Steady push (130 vs 89) and steady inject (128 vs 91) trail by ~1.4×. Both are dominated by one verified §4-deque push per element (~81 ns standalone); Viennot's hand-tuned buffer push is a few tens of ns cheaper inside their cadeque. Closing this means optimizing the kt4 extraction's push path — a §4 concern, orthogonal to the catenable layer, and the identified next target.

The model baseline (KT) tells the same story from the other side. Its cons-side cells (push/pop on a bare list) bound what any buffer can do, and its quadratic inject/eject cells are the price the production artifact was built to remove. Both extremes bracket KTf exactly where the mechanized buffer-primitive counts say it should sit.
