Pick a regime and press play. On the left is the deque right now: a stack of levels, each with a prefix and suffix buffer (0–5 cells), coloured by size (buf_color). On the right is the history: one row per level, painted left→right, so you can read the whole run at a glance. An overflow to size 5 spills a pair down to the next level — that downward carry is the recursive slowdown, and the reason a deeper level ticks at half the rate of the one above.
