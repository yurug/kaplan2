The colour invariants of Kaplan–Tarjan deques

An interactive illustration · kaplan2 · 2026-06-17 · companion to why-bounded-cascade.md

The whole trick in one sentence. Tag every buffer with a colour that depends only on how full it is, keep the chain regular (no two Reds adjacent), and a single bounded repair after each operation is enough to stay regular forever — that is what turns a purely-functional deque from amortised / O(log n) into worst-case O(1). This page animates the colour rule for the two data structures in this repo and shows the repair firing.

1 · Two structures, two colour rules

A buffer's colour is a pure function of its size. Red means an operation in some direction is about to over- or under-flow — it must be repaired. The warning bands (Yellow, and in §6 also Orange) are safe but one step from trouble. Green is safe in both directions. Press Play and watch the colour track the size; when a buffer turns Red, ensure_green fires and snaps it back to Green.

2 · The regularity invariant: no two Reds adjacent

The colour rule alone is not enough — you must also control how colours are arranged along the chain of levels. The invariant is:

No two Red levels are adjacent. Equivalently, between any two Reds there is at least one Green.

After every public operation a tiny routine, ensure_green, runs: if the top is Red, green_of_red rebuilds the top plus the first non-yellow level below it. Because the invariant guaranteed that level was not Red, the repair fires exactly once — no cascade. The long Yellow run in between is allocated as a single shared Packet, so re-threading the chain touches a constant number of cells, not one per yellow level. Press Step to walk through it.

What is faithful, and what is abstracted. The colour-of-size rule (panel 1) is exact — it is literally buf_color from Color.v. The chain animation (panel 2) shows the shape of ensure_green — one Red, one repair, the yellow Packet shared untouched — at the colour level; the precise buffer arithmetic of green_of_red's three cases lives in section4-repair-cases.md and the certified non-recursive ops in rocq/KTDeque/DequePtr/OpsKT.v.
